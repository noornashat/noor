Nissan Skyline

The Nissan Skyline is a line of compact cars, sports cars, and compact executive cars originally produced by the Prince Motor Company, a Japanese automobile manufacturer acquired by Nissan in 1966.

History

The Nissan Skyline has been in production since 1957. It has a long and storied history, with many different generations and models produced over the years.

The Skyline has been successful in many different forms of motorsport, including touring car racing, rallying, and drifting.

Nissan Skyline R34 GT-R

The Nissan Skyline R34 GT-R is a high-performance sports car that was produced from 1999 to 2002. It is powered by a 2.6-liter twin-turbo inline-6 engine that produces 276 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque.

The R34 GT-R's engine is known for its durability and reliability. It is capable of lasting for over 100,000 miles with proper maintenance.
